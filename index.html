Interview System — AV Sales Rep

Phase 1 · STAR Method — structured behavioral questions and scoring scorecard

Why meeting planners make ideal TRS sales reps — your opening pitch to the candidate
What they already have
✓ They know the client — event planners, associations, corporates
✓ They understand AV needs firsthand
✓ They have credibility: they've been on the buying side
✓ They have an existing network of event contacts
What changes for the better
✓ No more traveling city to city
✓ Unlimited commission — not a capped salary
✓ Selling to the same clients they already know
✓ Phone/virtual-first — not constant mingling
"You already know what these clients need — I'm just putting you on the other side of the table with a commission structure."
Green flags — hire signals
✓ Specific numbers in their answers
✓ Talks about following up consistently
✓ Comfortable saying "I don't know — I'd figure it out"
✓ Has chased down a client or deal before
✓ Shows curiosity about how AV works
✓ Asks about commission structure early
Red flags — pass signals
✗ Vague answers with no metrics or specifics
✗ Blames others when deals fell through
✗ Says "I'm not really a phone person"
✗ Can't describe a time they persuaded someone
✗ Only comfortable with warm/inbound leads
✗ Avoids talking about rejection or failure

Reference: What strong STAR answers look like
Transition script — use this to shift gears
"I appreciate the formal run-through. To be honest, I value my time — and yours — more than anything else today. I've got kids, a life, and a business that runs 24/7, so I like to skip the 'rehearsed' stuff and just talk about how we actually fit together."
Say this naturally. Let them exhale. The shift in energy is intentional — you want them off-script.

Life-cycle & operational flow questions
01
Excitement & Fit
"Think back to the job you actually enjoyed the most. What was the vibe? Was it the pace, the people, or the challenge?"
›
Hidden intent: Gauges if they thrive in high-stakes AV/rental environments or prefer a desk-bound routine. You want someone who lights up at "pace" and "challenge" — not "quiet" and "predictable."
✓ Green signals
Mentions energy, urgency, variety. Talks about being "in the mix." Uses words like fast-moving, hands-on, never the same day twice.
✗ Red signals
Says they liked it because it was "stable," "predictable," or they "knew what to expect every day." That's not this job.
Alignment note
02
Financial Floor
"Everyone has a 'happy' number — the salary where the bills are paid, you aren't stressed, and you aren't looking for the next exit. What is that number for you?"
›
Hidden intent: Prevents the revolving door. If their floor is higher than what's realistic in ramp-up months, they'll be stressed and gone before they hit stride. You need to know this up front.
✓ Green signals
Gives a specific, realistic number. Is transparent about their expenses. Doesn't hedge or inflate. Shows they've thought it through.
✗ Red signals
Refuses to name a number ("I'm flexible"). Gives a number that far exceeds base + realistic ramp commission. They're setting themselves up to bail.
Their "happy" number
Alignment note
03
Financial Ceiling
"Now — what is the goal number? Where do you want to be in 3 years to feel like you are truly exceeding your own goals?"
›
Hidden intent: Identifies ambition. You want someone whose 3-year number is achievable on commission — it keeps them hungry. If their goal is $200K and top performers here hit $75K, that's a mismatch you need to catch now.
✓ Green signals
Has a specific number with a rationale. Ties it to a life goal (house, family, debt-free). The number is ambitious but achievable with strong performance.
✗ Red signals
No target at all ("I'll just see how it goes"). Or a wildly unrealistic number that signals they don't understand commission-based comp yet.
Their 3-year goal
Alignment note
04
The 24/7 Reality
"We are a 24/7 industry. Sometimes clients email at 9 PM on a Saturday. Are you the type that needs a hard clock-out — which is fine — or do you prefer the flexibility to manage things as they pop up? I just need transparency."
›
Hidden intent: Aligns expectations early and filters out people who will silently resent the job. The phrase "which is fine" is deliberate — it disarms them so they answer honestly instead of telling you what they think you want to hear.
✓ Green signals
"I'm used to it." / "I treat my phone like a tool, not a leash." / Talks about managing their own schedule rather than watching a clock.
✗ Red signals
Immediately defensive about weekends or after-hours. "I have firm boundaries around my personal time." That's a legitimate value — just not for this role.
Availability vibe — their exact words
05
Self-Management Indicator
"If I gave you a goal and didn't check in on you for a week, what would I find on Friday afternoon — a finished project, a list of reasons why it couldn't be done, or a dozen questions in my inbox?"
›
Hidden intent: Identifies ability to self-manage and replicate performance without constant oversight. The three options in the question do the work for you — watch which one they laugh at, which they pause on, and what they say next.
✓ Green signals
Laughs and picks "finished." Then backs it up with a specific story unprompted. Acknowledges they might ask one or two smart questions early, but owns the result.
✗ Red signals
Immediately qualifies with "it depends on the project." Jokes that they'd have questions. Can't name a single time they drove something to completion alone.
Alignment note
06
Remote Reality · FORD Check
"Since this is a remote role, I'm big on transparency. I've worked from home and I know the drill — sometimes the dog barks or the kids need something. But at the end of the day, I need to know this seat is covered. What's your command center look like?"
›
Why this matters: Remote isn't a perk for this role — it's the operating model. You need to know if their home environment will support a 24/7 plugged-in industry, or if isolation and distraction will quietly erode their performance. FORD (Family · Occupation · Recreation · Dreams) gives you the full picture without a formal interrogation.
F · Family / Home Energy
"I'm a family man, so I get it. What's the energy of your home like during work hours — quiet and focused, or more of a circus?"
Listen for: Do they have a door they can close? Kids at home with no childcare? Constant interruptions described as normal? Partners who don't respect work time?
O · Occupation / Remote History
"Tell me about your last remote experience. What was the hardest part about staying productive when no one was looking over your shoulder?"
Listen for: Owned the struggle and solved it, or still blaming the environment? First-timer who sounds naive? Or a vet with real systems — dedicated space, set hours, clear rituals?
R · Recreation / Work-Life Line
"When the laptop closes, how do you separate work life from home life? Your office being 10 feet from your bed can burn you out fast — what's your reset?"
Listen for: Do they have any rituals, exercise, hard stops? Or does work just bleed into everything and they call that "dedicated"? Burnout in month 3 is expensive.
D · Dependability / Plan B
"In a 24/7 industry, if your internet goes down at 10 AM on a Tuesday, what's your Plan B? Hotspot, coffee shop nearby — or are you just offline?"
Listen for: Immediate, specific answer = problem-solver. Blank stare or "I'd call my provider" = clock-out mentality. This question separates operators from passengers.
